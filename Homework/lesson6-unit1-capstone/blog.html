The Heelflip & The Console: Learning from the Fall

Nov 9

Yo! It's Alex Rivera here. This summer, I'm working on two major "builds." One is my Unit 5 Capstone, and the other is mastering a clean heelflip on my board. You'd be surprised how much they have in common.

In class, we talk about how "every bug is a story" . When I'm at the skate park and I bail on a landing, I don't just get mad; I treat it like a JavaScript roadblock . I use a systematic "trial and error" approach. Was my weight too far back (a logic error)? Was my flick too slow (a timing function issue)? I have to form a hypothesis, adjust my feet, and try again until the "engine" of the trick runs smoothly. Whether I'm in the terminal or on the pavement, I'm learning that you can't reach the summit without a few diagnostics along the way !

Through the Lens: Architecting My Neighborhood Gallery

Oct 17

I've been spending my weekends working on a "Day in the Life" photo series featuring my neighborhood. As a digital architect, I don't just see a cool sunset; I see high-quality assets that need a professional home.

My goal is to take these shots and build a custom interactive image gallery. I'm moving away from a basic "Skeleton" and focusing on "Flexing the Grid" to make sure the layout is perfectly responsive on mobile and desktop. I've already got my images/ folder organized professionally—no "Div Soup" here! I want to make sure every photo has descriptive alt text so my site is accessible to everyone . It's one thing to take a great photo, but it's another thing to engineer an interface that lets the world experience it properly.

Fueling the Build: Ramen, Logic, and "Hello World"

Oct 3

People always ask what my "Debugging Ritual" is. It's pretty simple: a big bowl of spicy ramen and my DevTools open. There's something about the patience required to make authentic ramen broth from scratch that reminds me of writing impeccable form validation code. You can't rush the process if you want a professional result.

Looking back at my first "Hello, World" moment in Week 1, I realize I'm no longer just a consumer of the web . I'm the one wiring the electricity and plumbing behind the glass . My academic goal is to dive into Advanced UI/UX Design next, but for now, I'm just enjoying the Journey. From organizing thematic content in <section> tags to wiring the "Brain" of my site with JS, I'm officially building the web I want to see. Time to get back to the codebase—the ramen is getting cold!
